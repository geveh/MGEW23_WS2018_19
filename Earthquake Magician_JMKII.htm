Magician Michelle says that she can predict large megathrust earthquakes... well, at least 95% of the time. Assume that these earthquakes occur with a probability of 10^-5.How likely will Michelle cause a false alarm with her next prediction?
Given our scenario we have following probabilities:
  • # Probabilities
  • P_m <- 0.95 # Prob. Michelle is correct
  • P_mc <- 1-P_m # Prob. Michelle is wrong
  • P_e <- (10^-5) # Prob. of Earthquake occurance
  • P_ec <- 1-P_e # Prob. of NO Earthquake occurance
    • IN our scenario we have no earthquake ('Ec'), even though Michelle predicted one ('Mc').

    P(Mc | Ec)

    Resolving Bayes law we get:

    P(Mc | Ec) = (P(Ec | Mc) * P(Mc)) / ((P(Ec | Mc) * P(Mc)) + ((P(Ec | M) * P(M))

    M - Michelle Right, Mc - Michelle Wrong, E - Earthquake, Ec - NO Earthquake
  • # Calculating products of Posteriors
  • P_ec_mc <- P_ec * P_mc
  • P_ec_m <- P_ec * P_m
  • #Calculating wanted probability
  • P_mc_ec <- (P_ec_mc) / ((P_ec_mc) + (P_ec_m))
  • returnValue(P_mc_ec)
